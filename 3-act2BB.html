The Bunny Butcher

Act 2: System Anomaly

By Anthony Henson

I stand before a maintenance door armored like a vault. Its surface bites through my glove with icy hostility, stabbing the chill deep and numbing my fingers.

The card reader’s red eye blinks judgingly. Fumbling with my sweaty gloves, I bite one off and grasp my badge. The retractable cord snaps it right out of my fingers. Gritting my teeth, I snatch the card mid-air and swipe it. The console chimes: Elena Cross, Maintenance Tech, V-Tek LVL 4 . The badge photo is recent; it's not my best photo. My hair is a bit short, black, and curly, and I have green eyes. Could be worse, though.

The door groaned, grinding hard against the frame with a screech that set my teeth on edge. It slammed shut with a heavy thud, rattling right through my legs and through my chest.

I check my gray, thick, supposedly portable tablet with one hand, steadying it against my body. It's still hefty. I press the screen; it flickers, shows static, then flashes a green light across my visor. My breath fogs the visor, so I flip it up to get a clear look at the tablet. In big letters, it displays V-Tek. I scoff at the name; I don’t like it, but they practically own the city now and most jobs. I swipe the screen to pull up the latest logs.

The maintenance log says this area was sterilized last week, supposedly as clean as a hospital. Instead, the place is a wreck, an absolute biohazard. Sector 4 is largely a mystery to me, but from what I understand, it is a major laboratory that has been a critical part of the company's advancements and testing grounds. I squint, trying. to see what was wiggling in the shadows, but couldn’t. Either way, this place hasn't seen proper maintenance for months. I can't help but wonder if the morning crew is blowing off Sector 4?

Only one name comes to mind, my boss Nathan Gilford. His name alone makes my head ache, and my jaw hurt. He's usually in charge of Sector 4's maintenance. But here it says he still hadn't come back from his last job. He's still signed in. Between the freezing cold air from outside and the sudden chill down my spine, something must have happened here.

For the last few years, V-Tek has sent biohazard bags and large shipments down here, and the risks have only increased. Challenging higher-ups never worked; I either do the job or risk being terminated—I can’t risk it; my mother depends on me, as she isn’t able to live alone. Fear eats my gut each time one of these doors opens. But yet something roots to me. A duty I never chose, but can't leave. I remember so many nights staring at the ceiling, thinking about quitting. Wanting to just walk out, but every time I picture mom's face, her shaky hands when she fails to take her meds, it would be a lonely space if I never came back.

That's why it's my duty to do what I must. I'm always afraid, but I'm no coward. I can defend myself, even take things into my own matter if needed. But what would become of her if I failed?. I can hear her now: “Now, Elena, what did I say about taking risky jobs!” Something I've heard so many times in my life. And as much as I appreciate her, I am not a helpless little girl anymore. I rub my eyes with my left hand, then drag it down to my chin. “It’s gonna be another one of those, ain’t it?” I say aloud.

As I swing my heavy-duty flashlight through the fog, it sticks to me like shrink wrap. Wet. Sickly. Under my beam, the walls no longer look like concrete. They are swollen, bulging, shrinking as if the corridor is already trying to regurgitate me. Steel plates that once lined the walls are now strangled by throbbing red veins. Pipes fade under a growth. The floor is sticky and slimy with what appears to be sand. Lots of fine, white, powdery sand glistens, covering parts of the area.

"This isn't a power grid failure...", I mutter, as my grip tightens on the flashlight. "Just a standard protocol, quick change of a fuse....haha..." I say to bolster my confidence.

I step inside. The door slams shut, locking with a hungry, final click that seals me in like fresh meat.

I spin, heart hammering. Behind me, something sloshes—wet, sluggish—a sound that creeps up my spine and settles at my skull’s base.

A sheet of pink flesh oozes over the door, tendrils writhing and binding, sealing me in. No escape. Not now.

My hands tremble. I rip my V-Tek-issued tech radio from my belt, pressing it to my lips—craving a voice in the darkness.

“Dispatch, do you read?”

I wait. Seconds stretch; each feels a lifetime. The radio hisses, then crackles and pops, jerking me back to reality.

A familiar male voice replies: “Ahem-koff-koff, We read you. Please state your request.”

“Dispatch, about that call about a power failure you sent me on.”

Dispatch: “Come again, sorry you are—b—up.”

I quickly hit the talk button to reply. As I do, I look up at the ceiling, noticing that it is covered in filth as well. My grip on the flashlight tightens.

I smack the radio, annoyance boiling over in me. My flashlight sweeps the corridor ahead—the only way out, or deeper in.

One. by. one. Each of the tunnel's wall lights is engulfed by a never-ending darkness. Yet only one light shimmers, painting the floor with an oozing pink growth and squirming veins that crawl over the stained concrete, up the nearby walls, and along the rusted pipes. I bring the radio up one last time, up to my lips, my breath misting the casing with my thumb on the talk button. I hear the click of my actions echo down the desolate corridor.

“We need a containment team down in sector 4 access tunnels asap; do you read?” I say in an agitated voice.

I wait. Nothing. The air thickens, stale,heavy. It’s disrupted only by the wet squirm of tendrils behind me over the now blocked door. The radio is dead. Great.

Before I can move, I take a large breath. It burns in my lungs. I feel nauseated, on the edge of vomiting that tomato soup and grilled cheese from lunch. Nevertheless, I must continue.

Each footstep makes a terrible suction sound beneath my boots, like a hungry mouth beneath me. Near a destroyed security door, movement catches my eye in a pile of twitching flesh. I approach cautiously, gripping my propane torch at my side, and examine the object partially buried there: a broken, shattered flashlight, flimsy plastic, and useless. Turning it over in my gloved hand, I notice it seems recently dropped—still warm.

Something felt off. I looked back, but the dark corridor was empty. Ahead of me sat the mess. I poked it. Slimy,warm, and gritty. It recoiled from the contact. I pulled my hand back. I gag and grimace at a rather strong, pungent odor, very much like rotten fish. I bring my glove up an inch from my face. Rubbing some of the substance between my thumb and index finger, looking at it closer, it seems to be actively sweating white Quartz, otherwise known as silica."What the hell?’ I mumbled.

My stomach dropped. Silica isn't supposed to form like this—or ooze from anything alive. The night shift crew always joked about Sector 4 being cursed, about walls breathing and people quitting on the spot. I figured they were just messing with the new hires. Perhaps not. Some people say they could hear screaming below Sector 4 every once in a while, and odd clacking and clicking. Whatever this is, it isn’t good for the infrastructure; it's eating the metal as I speak.

With my curiosity satisfied, I rise to my full height of 5’4". I was the runt of the family. I am not tall by any measure, but when you spend most of your childhood in and out of hospital beds, you learn to put up a fight. Being the smallest in the room never stopped me. With grit in my gut and the resonances of my mother badgering me about risky actions, I press on.

As I go on down the infested corridor toward my next goal, the auxiliary station, every step plops under my feet. Awful, repetitive. Soon, I start to tune it out. I bring up my tablet. Check the blueprints—auxiliary is down the hall, right, then left. After, I clip my tablet to my right side and push forward.

I look around, shining my light through the thick air, and I notice a gouge in the flesh. My gut knots up. Something heavy dragged through the muck—hungry. Very hungry. Soon after, metal is bitten deep. Nearby, a wet, slow grinding metal sound drags, carving a gouge through the floor's raw flesh. I see a T-section in my path; the troubling sounds come from the right. To the left doesn't go where I need to go. My goal is to the right. With no real choice, I go right.

Keeping low, I follow the deep mark. Quite aware this means walking directly into a trap, but I have no real alternative; It's the only way to my objective. With resignation, I move cautiously.

“This is not part of my job description. I don’t get paid enough for this shit! I’ll have to demand—” As I tried to speak aloud, that sound returned again. It’s an excruciating sound, as if my brain is being very well scraped by tiny metal claws. It’s an aching pain going through my mind. I stumble a bit, catching myself to the side of the wall with the left hand holding my flashlight. The light curls downward. I look at the beam of light; it’s odd. It’s not— right. That isn’t how light quite works; it’s like something in the air is bending my light to the side. It’s incorrect.

Whatever is going on here is incredibly bad. I am no physicist, just a humble tech, but even the basic fundamentals tell me that my light shouldn’t be going to the right. I look back at my flashlight in my left hand, pointing straight down, yet it is pointing in the wrong direction. I lift off the wall and back to a healthy standing position. I squint my eyes and tilt my head in confusion.That’s… weird.

Despite the strangeness. I have a job to do, and this won't stop me. After recomposing myself, I started heading down again, following the deep mark left for me.

Clank. Squelch. The corridor inhales, exhales, seemingly alive at every part. My vision suddenly blurs a bit, my thoughts are a bit hazy, but I keep going. Ignoring the discomfort of my plight. I draw nearer and nearer to my task at hand. Restore power to the immediate area. My heart pounds regardless of what I ask of it. Knowing whatever is down here will come.

From the black, a sound breaks the silence. Not an emergency alarm as I expected. No. It is a distorted, warped, happy tune. It reminds me of the fairgrounds my mother took me to, but it’s leaking, skipping, from a dying speaker below. My skin crawls; I feel cold. It’s the feeling of worms lying underneath, wriggling inside me. Suddenly, a hot sensation emanates from my head. I touch my forehead. Sweat. The air conditioning mustn’t be working.

I don’t feel good; the sickness keeps getting worse. I feel like I am being pulled somewhere by a great, invisible force that I have yet to see. Dazed and still confused, I pull my grit, swiping the sweat, and move my now heavy-feeling legs forward. I can see the auxiliary door in my blurred-in and out vision. Need to get the power on.

Further pushing my now rather limpish body through the disgust ahead of me, I see a tall, slim, shadowed figure with long floppy-looking bunny ears down past the door. Like it is waiting. Daring me even. It holds something large that slowly comes towards me, causing the insidiously painful, metal-screeching, flesh-tearing sound at every step it takes.

I freeze like a deer in headlights for a few seconds. I look at the door, and I could maybe… no, the figure towards me is only maybe a foot away from the door now. My heart races for a solution. Quickly taking in my world. I notice there is another direction on my right; It’s another T-section. Without another thought, I hurried what amounted to a limping dog, out of breath and sweat dripping with vigor, down the unknown direction. I must… I must continue.

The corridor bends and snakes like I'm on an off-roading field trip. With each gasp of breath, it gets harder and harder to move forward. The sound of that scraping follows suit. I'm barely able to stand. Grabbing anything to give me the strength to move forward. I think of mom. What she’d do without me. The thought shudders me.

My pupil dilates. I think about how weak and helpless she would be without me; I can’t stand it. I'm the only one left. I’ve gotta come back from…this. She needs me. My clothing even feels like it's pressing me down now, and I can't even get up. I start lifting my ever heavier limbs from side to side. Ignoring the excruciating burning pain going through my nerves, I start crawling towards my goal.

That terribly distorted, muffled music still plays from below; A sudden flash appears in my mind, a large man in yellow overtop me. It gives me great pain. And the sound of a close train passing by. CLICK-CLACK, CLACK-CLACK. Still crawling towards my route before me, of the gunky corridor. I’m getting covered in the slimy grittiness of the flesh I am plop ploping in with every inch I crawl.

Fighting nothing more than peace and quietness, and the constant scraping piercing my ears forever pursuing me. I can finally see the door. Both the door and the tall person following are within reach of me. I see the card reader a few feet above me. I tremble, grab the ID, and struggle to stretch up to the reader to open my only hope. With every fumble was another chance to be laid to rest. I turn, seeing the towering thing above me.

The woman is wearing this crappy, homemade bunny costume. The patches are all different colors. The stitching is familiar, but I can’t put my finger on why; it reminds me of the antiseptic smell of a hospital.

The woman leans on a monstrous cleaver, as if at a kitchen counter. Her mask—a rabbit’s head, its ears big and droopy, its colors faded moves with every twitch and slight movement atop her shoulders. There’s something wrong in the way she stands; a wrongness that seeps into the room.

My breath catches. For a moment, smelling pungent, revolting old cloth, I hear my mother’s gentle humming. Then the memory sours. The comforting fragments are soiled by this figure before me, her blade standing upright between us.

I cannot move or speak. Panic blooms and recedes, leaving only a numb, ringing silence. In that stillness, memory and nightmare blur, and I am caught between them.

The shape in front of me glitches. For a fearful second, the darkness bleeds into a sickening, bright yellow. Synthetic fabric. There is a reek of cheap greasepaint that fills my throat, choking me. It's him, the large man. Grinning with a mischievous yellow-painted smile. "Hey boys, we have another here. She looks ‘fun’.”My lungs seize. The tunnel walls vanish, replaced by the red suit on his left, the slim blue one on his right. I can still feel the weight of them all on me. The cold ground of the dirt. The tearing, animalistic between my thighs. Accompanied by white-hot festering deep in me, thinking about it. Becoming a victim in the dark left behind.

Her odor jolts me back; it’s like rotten eggs. But I continue to stare, unable to look away. She raises the unbelievably large blade into my view from below. The door opened, which felt like it took a century. With every ounce of energy I had, I launched myself into the room, CLANG, QUELSH, I hear barely within an inch of my life going down the gutter. I look upwards and press a red button above me with a heavy thump. The door releases and closes quickly, making a loud thud.

Shortly after, I see her in a nearby window pane next to the door, pressing her hand against it, then looks right directly through me. The mask has large, black, glossy button eyes sewn to it and wears a creepy, open smile. She stays for a short bit, like she is silently saying you're inevitably doomed. But something grabbed her attention. A pitter-patter of footfalls on the other side of me. The twisted bunny backs away, leaving a smudge against it, and moves slowly to the right like a predator creeping up on its prey, dragging that massive knife behind it.

Gasping for air, I shift to my side and prop myself up by my elbow. The floor is hard and cold, but thankfully, there is none of that filth here. I look around the room; all I see is a control panel and a screen above displaying basic statistics on the current area’s power usage and auxiliary power. With great effort, I hoisted myself up and lumbered towards the controls. I lean myself over, holding on to the top to recover. Sweat runs off my face and drips onto the faceplate.

I take a moment to reorient myself, still in disbelief that any of this is even happening to me. Why me? Why now? I rub my temples and my face in frustration while rubbing some of the gritty silica off me. Then I look at the screen directly in front of me.

It’s an older-looking console with a wide rectangular base and a large, rounded rectangular screen above. The room is filled from top to bottom with dust, oil, and grime, but no sign of the pink flesh was seen. I breathe out a sigh of relief. That muffled, distorted music is also gone. The screen displays Power Aux: 0%.

I look down at the faceplate; it’s strange, off-putting, and nothing that I’ve ever seen before in my life. The keys have labels I can’t comprehend, and the knobs vary in size with no repeated pattern. Each switch is marked in gibberish, except for a single large green button plainly labeled “Power.” “Couldn’t make it obvious enough, I guess,” I mutter. I press the button—CLICK. Nothing happens. I try again. CLICK. Still nothing. CLICK CLICK CLICK. The room remains silent, except for the faint hum of a nearby vent.

Frustrated, I step back. Out of the corner of my eye, I notice a sticky note beneath the console’s access panel at the base. I kneel and squint at the message: “fix me.” geez, way to go, guys. Typical, leave a broken machine for the next shift. I sigh while shaking my head. It’s always Nathan who pulls this crap.

The panel is held in place by four large bolts. This should be simple. I fish a socket wrench out of my tool belt. The first one does not fit. I try a second; still no luck. The third finally fits, and I set to work. Each turn of the bolt squeals through the room.

After much time unscrewing each bolt, which took more effort than I wished. Wasted a good half an hour, probably. Those bolts were on there tight. Beyond tighter than they should have been. strange. After much time, I just realized. When did I pick up wrenches? I don’t remember seeing them when I first arrived. I must have picked them up earlier without realizing it. “Just a habit, I suppose. I do tend to pick up things without noticing.” I say in a low voice, not to draw attention to myself more than I probably have.

Pushing the strangeness aside, I inspect the compartment's insides. They seem completely disconnected, as if deliberately sabotaged. Them damn fools, playing around like this. What gives! My face contorts into a grumpy, annoyed expression while reaching into the small box full of internals ripped out like guts dangling around. Blue, Yellow, Green, Black, Magenta. There are many colored wires in the cavity that are in dire need of repair. I take one and stare at it. I grab my flashlight and shine it inside the metal body. Thankfully, they are color-coded, but it seems all the switches are connected in series. Which means…I need to connect all the switches so the “Power” button works. Who even engineer’d this crap?”This doesn’t even make sense! Fix, Fix,” I say loudly, realizing I uttered it out without a thought. I cover my mouth as if it would prevent the loud noise I just made from being heard.

Stillness still only happens with the occasional sound of pipes hissing; time feels like coldness along my skin as I work diligently on what is an extension of a larger machine. I get caught up in a task that feels like a continuous loop. ZAP! I grimace in pain from accidentally touching a powered wire to the ground. “Don’t touch the sides of the maze.” Kind of shit; whoever engineered this is a real piece of work. Maybe even perhaps a bit of a masochist, with such a poorly designed installation. As I attach and turn the last wire into its rightful place, the massive green “power” button lights up and starts blinking, emitting a mild hum. Now that is a noise I like. The sound is pleasurable, in a way that feels fulfilling and touches my heart.

“Elena, oh god, thank god it’s you, Elena, please open the door,” he says in a desperate voice as he pounds on the window. For some reason, I can’t move; I want to help but am… frozen. My breathing becomes irregular. My thoughts are hazy, and it’s hard to think a little bit. I try to reach for the button, but suddenly. “Elena, open the goddamn do—” a large gargling sound can be heard, and the sound of flesh streaking down the window. I just stare and watch in horror at what was once Nathan Gilford, now just nothing but a split-in-half hot dog sliding down the side. Getting “ketchup” everywhere like it was overloaded.

Hungover, feeling like I could puke at any moment's notice. I back away from the door, and I take in my local area for anything of interest. Then I remember. I remember hearing the air moving in the room. I focus on a rather high-up vent. Then I look back down at the flashing green light; I scramble to push it, but it doesn’t do anything. Oh right, I need to flip all the switches. I flip all of them in quite a hurry and press the now-solidly lit button, slamming it with a hard fist. A loud sound builds slowly as the power bar fills. Clonk, Clonk. I hear from the right side. Clang, Clang. Then a rather mechanical straining sound comes from the right-side door. A sharp, large object bursts through the slits of the door! She’s…using it as leverage! Oh no…This isn’t good. I look back over to the left door, but it seems to be locked from the outside now.

Now breathing heavier than I was even doing beforehand, I keep racing to the vent from above and back to the door in the now rather suffocating room; I could fit in it; it’s pretty large, and I am rather thin and short. But how… do I get up there? The console is too far from that side of the wall. While during that time of thinking of my means of escape from what is most certainly the end if I don’t act. The whining of the door motor strains from the break-in. The blade is tall. I can use it to my advantage to reach the vent. I'm rather athletic; I did track during high school and was a cheerleader. Despite my disapproval, mother. It only took a bit for them to give. It tried its best, but the clearly insane amount of such force was no match for such a clearly inhuman creature. The door opens with a grinding sound, giving up all the life it once had.

The tall, slim figure crosses the threshold, standing roughly six feet tall, her outline devouring the little light that leaked through. My pursuer comes into clearer focus. The form-fitting suit is a pale, rottin' dermis that illuminates beneath the cold-colored flashing lights above. A roaring sound comes from somewhere. Unfazed, the figure raises her massive cleaver overhead, then swings it in a powerful, horizontal arc toward me. The blade cuts through the air with a SWOOSH, striking metal and the ceiling in rapid succession, CLANK, THONK, CLANK.

I barely had time to dodge such an attack, nearly not clearing it from my head, clipping a small curly stray hair. I could see the reflection of my face as it went past me, as if time froze for a split second. That was incredibly close, too close to my liking. My heart throbs and my adrenaline kicks in hard. There is no way I can fend off such an inhuman thing. The bunny twists its head to the side at a disgustingly 90 degrees that no human could possibly manage, and twitches a bit; her large, floppy bunny ears follow. Clearly annoyed, CLANG, SCREEEECH, that was the sound of another attempt to cut me down, but I managed to nearly avoid it once more. Finally, she heaves another attack at the ready. I position myself just above the vent so I can direct her attack to my advantage. I’m not dying today. I don’t plan on it. SWOOSH, the sound of the final deciding attack is coming my way. I roll to the left, avoiding it. THUMP THUMP THUMP. That is her pounding one of her paws against the floor. She is truly pissed off that I keep avoiding her attacks. In a rather demanding walk, Stabs the large, dragged knife cleaver directly in the ground in front of me. I stumble slightly, and my legs give, but thankfully, I catch myself by the elbow.

The bunny creature stands over me in a menacing manner and then gently crouches down on top of me like that of a compassionate lover. Her bunny head mask is a few inches from my face. As if she is staring straight into the pit of my soul. My semblance is seen in myself when I look at my glossy black mirror,like large button eyes. My hand slowly moves towards the propane torch on my belt. The coarse fabric hand caresses my light brown face up and down and then traces the line of my jaw, almost lovingly. She then says in a rather cute voice from the suit.”my, my, what a wonderful… skin you have.” With a pause that seemed like forever, other than with a slight heaving sound coming from her. Suddenly, she tilts her head a bit and says. “You look…familiar… Do I… know you?”

Now is the best time; she's off guard. My fingers grip the handle of my propane torch. I hammer the igniter, and a roaring, brilliant blue heat erupts from the end. I shove it into her face with a mighty thrust. The fabric burns and rolls up, falling apart onto the ground. She jerks back and stumbles with her hands to her face, making a shrieking sound away from me. She scrambles, trying to put the fabric back onto her. In a pissed-off, menacing voice, “You ruined it! You ruined it! Gonna have to use yours instead! fix, fix!”

While she is distracted, I vault upwards, relying on my cheerleading skills, and precariously stand on the massive knife at the top she left behind. From this angle, I am now taller than her. I look down and see beneath the torn fabric: a mechanical metal frame intertwined with squirming, sinewy flesh, blistered and burned. Her bunny mask, covering her face, is half-burned on the right side; its seams curl at the scorched edges, exposing dead, artificial green eyes. Forcing myself to focus, I tear my gaze and quickly look at the duct above—my way out. I plant one foot firmly on the flat surface of the knife to steady myself. I pull it vigorously, loosening the already loose vent. I hurriedly threw it down. Clink. Seizing my opportunity, I grab the edge of the vent. Now hanging on the edge of the gaping mouth that will swallow me fully. I haul myself up and squeeze into the narrow opening, pulling myself inside before she can react.

She is no longer distracted; I hear a SSHUNK sound, sharp and sudden, as though she is unsheathing her knife from the ground like a sword. I continue crawling through the vent; it snakes a bit as it goes. The giant knife suddenly bursts through the vent wall right in front of me, the metal rupturing with violent force. I barely stop behind it; it's right up in my face. My reflection stares at me again. The inhumanly large knife is pulled out, leaving a gaping flanged hole. The monster peeks inside and then says loudly, "I'm herrrre!" I continue forward once again, this time hurrying a little faster until I reach a vertical drop; a downward shaft interrupts my path. I peeked over the edge to see how far down the drop went. Then—CLANG. That massive metal shard slammed back into the duct, missing my spine by inches. I flinched, my slick hands slipped right off the molding, and I just fell. Straight down into the black.

I land with a hard thump, my vision becomes hazy and blurry, like I have some kind of film over my eyes. My head is thumping with pain. My eyesight doubles and fades away as I fight my heavy lids. Everything goes dark.

* * *

Though the world slowly fades in, I feel the shivering of cold concrete. Awaking below a cold blue glow above me. I take a swift sitting, being propped up with my hands. Looking around, it’s a rather secluded area; the walls are concrete, and there are no windows to speak of, other than a single entry to leave and another familiar-looking strange console. I briefly look behind me; I see pipes lining the walls, like veins into a void. I did it. “He, he.” I really did it, I actually got away. Filled with gusto and determination, with a big smile, I jump up feeling decent. Empowering even. I’ve never felt this good before. Looking at the foreign-looking console and screen. Several pieces of footage are displayed on the ugly display, which is covered in pink, fleshy gunk.

On one feed, hazmat workers with 'V-Tek' emblem’d on their suits, holding flamethrowers, are directing fire at the creature. The thing is monstrous, yet its reaction is much human-like: it shrivels and recoils from the fire, terrified, unable to defend itself. The flames burn away patches of organic tissue and burn the tentacles to ash. It emits agonizing shrieks that rise every time. Its crying evokes the anguish of a helpless animal. A sound with so much suffering behind it. An inexplicable wave of sorrow flows over me. My eyes tear up and start pooling at my feet. Drip drip drip. My watery eyes create. A great deal of empathy wells up within me, as if I could feel the pain myself.

The next camera feed shows a wreck. It's labeled V-Tek Lab 5, and it's buried under a disgusting mess of meat and tentacles. A few are sagging down from the ceiling like cables. I think about changing it. Conveniently, it flipped the feed away from it. I'm pretty relieved from that. Next, a glitchy animatronic show appears, blasting some electronic beat that rattles my mind. The audio is poor, but even through the static, I can make out a few lyrics. “'Cause I could eat you like an animal… I’m so cannibal… starvin’ now…” I see something moving around in the corner. I look there, and the camera pans to provide a better view and focus.

Huh? That's odd. That is the 2nd time that happened… It's a humanoid robotic rabbit on a single wheel, running around to service empty tables. The rabbit waitress wears a retro-looking "Big Roni's Pizzacade" apron nearby, with pizzas dropped on the floor. I think about focusing on her more, and the camera does as I ask. The area seems to be some kind of automated restaurant or underground exhibit.

The room before me shifts and resets itself, zooming forever in intervals, its dimensions and contents distorting in ways that aren't possible. My mind reflects upon this disorientation, becoming tangled and fragmented. The air has a faint tinge of electricity, like an invisible grid moving and fuzzy around me. I feel like just static in a larger system, or maybe my own mind is just fracturing from exhaustion and stress. Either I am manifesting supernatural abilities, or I am succumbing to a vivid hallucination from overwork. I raise my hands up in front of me and stare at them, expecting them to burst into flames or melt. Yet they don't before me.

Suddenly, the screen goes blank and shows an unprompted search bar. The page looks a bit outdated. At the very top, it says V-Tek archive server. A faint, metallic tang fills the air, and I notice the gentle hum deepening inside the console, the vibration felt through my fingertips.
